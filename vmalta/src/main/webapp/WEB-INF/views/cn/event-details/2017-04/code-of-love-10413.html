CODE OF LOVE

05/04/2017 - 13/04/201709:00 - 17:00

Feminine Art - delicate, sensitive and sensuous, yet persistent and enduring - transmits the pulsations of society, evoking emotion and controversy, while also kindling temperate and refined human warmth. More often than not, it advocates good morale and enlightens one’s mind and soul.

Looking back at the original Silk Road route, along which this new type of fabric was transported, it can be deduced that this textile was highly favoured by the maidens of Rome for its smooth texture, soft feel, bright colours and sheen. While swaggering down the street, their thin light-weight silk clothing would define the outline of their beautiful, graceful figures. For a time, Feminine Art was one of the most influential art genres in Rome. However, it aroused apprehension amongst the Roman Senate, who regarded it to be too unrestrained, stirring considerable dispute: Should one’s clothing not fully hide the figure of the human body and impart a solemn impression. Otherwise, how could it be called clothing? Thus this predominant fashion was relatively short-lived. Nevertheless, it was argued that appreciation of beauty is intrinsic to human nature; that it is essentially a human right to wear whatever one pleases; that the insight, perception and expression of beauty associated with the female gender often excels beyond that of their male counterpart ….. Perhaps this is where the charm of feminine art lies.

The Silk Road was a platform for cross-regional, cross-ethnic, and cross-cultural exchanges, so that this cross-boundary exchange led to heterogeneous fusion, penetration, activation, and evolution. The purpose of the world tour of Chinese contemporary feminine art is to consider and discuss this genre in an international context, and foreground its resonance and differences in relation to those of other countries. Dialogue with female artists from countries along the Silk Road trade route is especially effective in the exploration of intercultural blending between Chinese and foreign cultures. It reveals the benign intention behind the notion of “appreciating the beauty possessed by one's self and others, while also sharing this beauty and harmony with the world”. Therefore, the series of activities which constitute this world tour serve as continuation and extension of the journey initiated by encounters of diverse civilizations along the Silk Road, in the hope that mutual interaction and cross-pollination between different civilizations will strengthen exchange between Chinese culture and other exotic cultures. It is also hoped that that the unique narrative of feminine artists’ paintings, will shed light on ancient Chinese legends as well as demonstrate China`s contemporary style and sentiment, so that the spirit of the Silk Road can be revitalized by the process of spreading modern Chinese civilization.

Culture, after all, is a representation of people`s lifestyles, their wisdom for survival, and the value they attribute to life. Although this world tour focuses on works by contemporary female artists that are actively engaged in the field of Chinese contemporary art, the “gender identity” is actually just a narrative strategy for social development and changes in these modern times. It is hoped that Chinese contemporary feminine art can inspire interest in its survival, and the ideological, emotive, and idealistic pursuits inherent to this art form, as well as trigger deliberation on China`s development and progress, and uphold humanism in the fabric of Chinese society.  This world tour highlights work of Chinese contemporary female artists of different ages, styles, and levels. Some of them are frank and innocent, others miserable and melancholic; some are sensational and fascinating, while others are rustic and calm; or hazy and mysterious, if not simply straightforward and blunt. They are not bound by any stereotypes, but rather demonstrate unprecedented audacity, novelty and self-consciousness in their artistic narrations. They also have a broader international perspective, unwavering confidence in their cultural identity, and are more open-minded and inclusive. Their artistic concepts of writing or painting embody both the acknowledgement of the cultural heritage of their predecessors, and also the pursuit of the rebirth of traditional artistry.  Chinese contemporary female artists strive to make a notable difference in the reshaping of gender-building mechanism forged under the influence of diverse cultures, and the restructuring of concepts and reinforcement of values strained by the impact of consumer society, with a view to rebalancing the cultural evolution brought forth by the information revolution.

The Silk Road Culture world tour - Chinese Contemporary Feminine Art Exhibition is not only an activity for international cultural exchange, but also an opportunity for everyone to gain a new perspective on Chinese feminine art. It highlights the peerless elegance of the Chinese female, with the more recently acquired sense of self-esteem, self-confidence, self-reliance, and self-improvement. It is believed that this world tour will be a new page in the history of Chinese feminine art.
